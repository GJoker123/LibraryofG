<p>&ldquo;Go to sleep honey. See you in the morning.&rdquo;</p>
<p>And with a kiss and a quick tuck, the mother left her son in bed, anxious to check her television to make sure she didn&rsquo;t miss her Come dine with me special. Before leaving she made sure the light was off on her way out.</p>
<p><strong>&ldquo;Click!&rdquo;</strong></p>
<p>Enveloped in darkness the boy lie awake, worried and scared as his imagination began to expand and unleash against his will. Released from their cage, the nightmares were free to torment the boy - as they would do most nights. Fortunately for him, they never lasted long, disappearing from his mind late into the night. Furthermore, the nightmares were becoming less frightening &ndash; maybe this was due to the boy getting older or the nightmares becoming predictable in their scare tactics or a combination of both. Regardless, it seems the boy was going to be finally rid of these ghastly creatures that haunted his dreams and experience peaceful nights from now on.</p>
<p>&nbsp;<strong>&ldquo;Thud!&rdquo;</strong></p>
<p><strong>&ldquo;Thump!&rdquo;</strong></p>
<p>&nbsp;A sudden bang could be heard from the room behind his wall. His mother&rsquo;s. It was possible that she may have dropped something and as he heard footsteps coming round to his room he felt reassured that she was going to tell him everything was alright.</p>
<p>That was until he saw the hand that opened his door.</p>
<p>Holding the bloodstained hammer.</p>
<p>Belonging to a face that grinned at him with malice.</p>

<p>&nbsp;<strong>But the monsters didn&rsquo;t.</strong></p>
